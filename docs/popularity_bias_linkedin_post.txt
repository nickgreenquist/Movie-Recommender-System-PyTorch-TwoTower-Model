I asked my movie recommender what a WW2 movie buff should watch next.

It returned (two) The Lord of the Rings movies and six of its ten picks weren't even war movies — they were just massively popular.

That's popularity bias: a common recsys feedback loop. Popular titles get shown more → get more clicks → model learns to show them more. Unchecked, almost every recommender collapses into a 'Most Popular' shelf.

The fix (Menon et al., 2020): during training, add α·log(popularity) to every movie's score (one line change). Inference isn't affected and there is zero added latency.

Same WW2 Movie fan:
Before: The Return of the King, Braveheart, Gladiator, The Godfather Part II. Not a WW2 movie among them.
After: Cross of Iron, Tora! Tora! Tora!, Battle of Britain. Actual war films.

In exchange for a ~4% accuracy drop (which fell only on already-popular movies), the share of my entire catalog recommended to eval users jumped from 48% → 84%.
